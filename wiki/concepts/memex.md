# Memex

A hypothetical device described by Vannevar Bush in his 1945 essay "As We May Think." A personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents.

## Connection to the LLM Wiki Pattern

Karpathy cites the Memex as the intellectual ancestor of the [[concepts/llm-wiki-pattern]]:

> Bush's vision was closer to this than to what the web became: private, actively curated, with the connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves. The part he couldn't solve was who does the maintenance. The LLM handles that. (source: karpasky.md)

The key parallels:
- **Private and personal** — not a public resource, but your own curated collection
- **Associative trails** — connections between documents are first-class data (equivalent to `[[wiki-links]]`)
- **Active curation** — not a passive archive, but actively maintained and organized

The key difference: Bush envisioned a mechanical device operated by the user. The LLM wiki pattern automates the maintenance that makes the system actually sustainable.
