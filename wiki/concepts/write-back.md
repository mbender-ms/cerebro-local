# Write-Back

The practice of filing valuable query answers, syntheses, comparisons, and analyses back into the wiki as new pages. Part of the [[concepts/query]] operation in the [[concepts/llm-wiki-pattern]].

## Why It Matters

> "Write-back is the single most important part." — bitsofchris (2+ years running the pattern at 4,000+ notes) (source: karpasky.md)

Karpathy mentions this almost in passing, but community practitioners report it's the key to compounding value. Without write-back, valuable insights disappear into chat history. With it, every research session, every synthesis, every new connection the agent finds grows the knowledge base. (source: karpasky.md)

## What to Write Back

- Comparison pages generated from queries
- Analysis and synthesis across multiple sources
- New connections discovered between concepts
- Key ideas extracted from AI chat conversations
- Active work stream tracking

## Best Practices

- Make it clear which content was human-written vs LLM-written (source: karpasky.md, comment by bitsofchris)
- Limit the agent to write to specific locations to keep things organized
- The knowledge base should grow through **use**, not just ingestion (source: karpasky.md)
